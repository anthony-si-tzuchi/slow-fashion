Wasted Wardrobes

The fast fashion industry has created an environmental crisis. This is the story of what happens to the billions of garments produced each year after they leave the shelf.

A Global Mountain of Waste

92 Million Tons

Of textile waste are generated globally every single year. That's equivalent to a garbage truck full of clothing being landfilled or incinerated every second.

The Fate of Our Clothes

Once discarded, very little textile waste is managed sustainably. The overwhelming majority is either buried in landfills or burned, representing a massive loss of material value and a significant environmental burden.

An Alarming Trend: Landfills on the Rise

Data from the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency shows a dramatic and sustained increase in the amount of textile waste sent to landfills over the past several decades. This trend highlights the unsustainable trajectory of linear consumption.

The Plastic in Our Wardrobes

The majority of modern clothing is made from synthetic, plastic-based fibers like polyester and nylon. These materials are cheap to produce but are non-biodegradable and shed microplastics with every wash.

Top Global Textile Waste Generators

While fashion is a global industry, waste generation is concentrated. Industrial and consumer powerhouses lead the world in producing the largest volumes of discarded textiles annually.

The Linear Lifecycle of Fast Fashion

The core of the problem lies in the industry's linear "take-make-dispose" model, which prioritizes speed and volume over durability and circularity, inevitably leading to massive waste.
